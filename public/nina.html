I hope this guide provides the clearest way to communicate what I'm aiming for. I tried a few different approaches but found they only made things more complicated. As you've heard me say — keep the thing about the thing. This is my attempt to do exactly that.

A question for you — please give me your instinct

Do you think the acronyms should be language-specific rather than remaining in English when translated into German and other languages?

My instinct is that they should be created in the reader's native language. Since the system works more through meaning and nuance than the specific words themselves, I feel locally developed acronyms would be more natural, memorable, and personally meaningful.

For example, if someone were learning the PC1 system in German, would it feel more authentic and impactful if the acronym was built from German words rather than using English words like C.R.A.F.T. or S.P.E.A.K.?

Am I overthinking this — or would you personally prefer native-language acronyms in a translated edition? I'm asking now because I'd rather make the right decision before we proceed than change direction later.

[TITLE CARD — spoken] PromptCraft 1. Teaching Machines to Listen. By John Kenneally. [INTRO — spoken] PromptCraft is related to prompt engineering — but the two are not the same. Prompt engineering is largely technical. PromptCraft is personal. It is built around the way you think, the words you use, and the outcomes you actually need. The system is driven by a linguistic framework called C.R.A.F.T. — Context, Role, Action, Format, Tone — five structured elements that govern every interaction between a person and an AI system. This is not a book for people who simply want to use AI better. There are countless tools and systems that already do that, in countless ways. PromptCraft 1 offers something more specific: the ability to shape AI responses the way you want them shaped — in any situation, any context, any day. More precisely. More efficiently. More personally. And with far greater control. For your studies. For your work. For the organisations and communities you belong to. Using your own language as the foundation of what the system returns. This book explains what each element of C.R.A.F.T. does, why it works, and how to apply it across any field or discipline.
